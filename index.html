About L6FC

Lowestoft Sixth Form College is an outstanding £25 million purpose-built college. Our science laboratories, art rooms, music and multi-media suites and performance spaces have all been specially designed to support your learning. Additionally, over £1.3 million has been invested in the IT infrastructure and equipment alone to provide an exceptional service. We have full WIFI coverage.

The college is a very friendly and welcoming place. We are bigger than a school sixth form so can offer many courses and combinations whilst we are small enough to offer real, individual attention and support. With us you will gain vital qualifications and something extra too: the confidence and inspiration to excel.

We are now a well-established college offering an excellent student experience; we truly value our students and listen to their contributions. In return, we ask that you commit to your studies and work hard. Our teachers are passionate about your learning. They want you to enjoy their subject and to succeed. Every student is unique and our support recognises this. The transition from school can seem scary, but you will have a Student Achievement Mentor who you will meet every week to support you. You will be able to monitor your progress on the Lowestoft Sixth Form College Dashboard. You will also work alongside your teachers and Student Achievement Mentor on agreeing individual targets which will be continually monitored throughout the year. We offer a host of other support services all to enable you to achieve the best you can.

We offer you many, many opportunities beyond your studies. You will make friends, develop new interests and have a fresh start. These experiences will both enrich you and your time at Lowestoft Sixth Form College but also enhance your CV and support your university application.

Computer Science

This exciting course will demystify the magic of computer systems hardware and software, providing you with a good understanding of the current issues facing computer scientists and the computing industry.

You will be introduced to the internal workings of the central processing unit, learn how data is exchanged, find out how to create software, develop your knowledge of data types and explore legal and ethical issues.

You will also develop your problem-solving and computational thinking skills, as well as the ability to develop algorithms to describe and solve problems. You will demonstrate your practical abilities through completing a programming project that is chosen by you.

Mathematics

Do you enjoy Maths at school? Do you like solving problems? Do you feel confident with the work you have met so far at GCSE? If so, you should take A Level Maths.

Maths is a demanding and challenging subject, but it can be extremely rewarding if you are prepared to put in the time and effort.

Maths has wide applications in areas such as engineering, business, finance, science, computing and technology. In fact, there continues to be a national shortage of qualified mathematicians, so this course will give you a competitive edge with future employers.
